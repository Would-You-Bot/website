## What Does an Active Community Look Like?
An active community can look different depending on your server's niche. If you're a streamer or content creator, it's a space where your audience actively discusses your content, listens for updates, and participates in discussions regularly. For gamers, an active community takes the form of an active gathering of players finding teammates, discussing strategies, and sharing experiences. And if you're a developer, an active community is a space for feedback on projects, collaborating on projects, and staying up to date with trends and new technologies.

However, an active community isn't just for specific niches. For companies and organizations, it's a space for customer interaction, allowing a brand to build its community.
## How can I make my Discord server active?

You might know the following scenario all too well: You've created a Discord server, but it's not very active. You're wondering how to make your Discord server active and what you can do to get more people in there. Well, we have some tips for you!

### 1. Make Your Members Feel Welcome

The first step to increase activity within your Discord server is to ensure your members feel genuinely welcomed. You mightâ€™ve seen or heard about a dedicated welcome channel with a bot generated message. While this may seem like a decent approach, it's not necessarily the most effective method for providing a more personal greeting. Instead, consider personally welcoming new members in your general chat. This helps to create a more inviting atmosphere and increases the likelihood of them becoming active and engaged members of your server.

### 2. Be Active Yourself

We had an opportunity to chat with Howl, a Discord Partner and the Owner of [Pottercord](https://discord.gg/pottercord), as well as TonyBytes, a former Discord Partner and Certified Moderator. Both of these experts hit on the same basic advice when it comes to fostering a more active Discord server: the owner's active involvement in the community. This is because members are more likely to stay and engage when they witness the server owner's active presence and genuine care for their community. Inactivity on your part may lead members to believe that you are disengaged from the Discord server, which may leave them to leave.

### 3. Use Engagement Bots

Engagement bots can add a lot of value to your server. They can help with keeping your server active by providing fun activities for your members to participate in. There's a wild variety when it comes to engagement bots, with each one having its own unique features. 

- One of these Discord engagement bots is [Would You](https://wouldyoubot.gg/invite), which happens to be our own bot. Would You is a Discord bot that allows you to play would you rather, never have I ever, higher or lower, and what would you do on discord. It's a great way to break the ice and get people talking.

- Another great bot is [Tiny Games](https://top.gg/bot/878580419736506388), which offers a wide variety of fun and entertaining games for you to play with your friends on Discord, such as Rock Paper Scissors, Connect Rour and Tic Tac Toe. It's a great way to have fun with your friends and keep your server active.

- If your members enjoy chatting for multiple hours a day, [Countr](https://countr.xyz/) may be a perfect addition. Easy to setup, Countr can create and manage counting channels within your server. Not to mention it also offer a leaderboard and customization for how a counting channel is setup.

- Another great option for more anime centric servers is [Mudae](https://top.gg/bot/432610292342587392). With a database of 95,000 waifus and husbandos, members can collect and build unique card collections. One of the best features offered is the vast variety of chat based minigames, perfect for server events or casual 1v1s.

While we mentioned only a few of the top engagement bots, there's still plenty more out there that you can find!
### 4. Host Events

Hosting events can make your server more active by giving your members something to look forward to. Events can be anything from a movie night to a game night to a karaoke night. The possibilities are endless! Hosting events is a great way to get your members involved and keep them active in your server.

One event we used to do a lot was a 'finish my art' competition. It was held on a weekly basis and the community would vote on who finished the art the best. The winner would get a special role and a month of Discord Nitro or Discord Nitro Basic. It was a great way to get people involved and keep them active on the server while giving back to the community.
